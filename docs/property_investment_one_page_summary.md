# Property Investment One-Page Summary (Income-Based)

## Direct answer
Based on your current household pre-tax income of $300,000, the practical target is:

- **0 to 1 property** if you keep your full projected lifestyle spending (including travel) unchanged.
- **1 to 2 properties** only if you actively run a lower discretionary-spend mode (especially travel) and focus on higher-yield new builds.
- **Do not target 3+ properties** at current income/settings. Debt and serviceability likely cap you before that.

## Inputs used for the calculation

- Household pre-tax income: **$300,000**
- Estimated after-tax income used: **$192,862**
- Average projected annual spend (including travel): **$185,674**
- Average projected annual core non-travel spend: **$138,958**
- New-build reference property: **$700,000**
- Deposit + purchase costs assumption: **25.5%** (20% deposit + 5.5% costs)
- Debt per property (80% LVR): **$560,000**
- Interest rate: **6.2%** (serviceability stress at 9.2%)
- Non-interest property costs: **1.2% p.a.**
- Principal repayment: **1.5% p.a.**
- Gross yield assumptions: **4.5% new build**, **3.7% established**
- Marginal tax used in model: **39%**

## What the math says

### 1) Borrowing and serviceability limits

- Debt-to-income limit at 5x income = **$1.5m debt capacity**.
- At $560k debt per property, this implies **2 properties max by DTI**.
- Serviceability test at stressed rates also comes out to about **2 properties max**.

### 2) Cashflow drag per property (annual)

- New build (with salary-offset loss treatment): **-$15,488** per year
- Established (year 1 only salary-offset): **-$18,904** per year
- Established (post-quarantine): **-$25,620** per year

This means established property becomes materially harder to carry once negative gearing quarantine bites.

### 3) Surplus capacity against your budget

- If full projected total spending is maintained, average annual surplus is only about **$7,188**.
  - This does **not** comfortably support even one negatively geared property.
- If you run a core-spend mode (travel/discretionary flexed down), average annual surplus is about **$53,904**.
  - This can support **1 to 2 new-build properties** from a cashflow perspective, while debt/serviceability still cap near 2.

## Recommended property path

1. **Phase 1: Buy 1 property only** (new build, but not generic oversupplied stock).
2. **Hold 12–24 months and re-test** with actual rent, costs, and interest rates.
3. **Only add property #2** if all are true:
   - Household surplus remains above **$35k-$45k** after actuals.
   - You can keep an emergency/offset buffer of at least **9–12 months** core spend.
   - Portfolio still passes stressed serviceability at current lending settings.
4. **Avoid a 3rd property target** unless income rises materially and cashflow improves.

## What type of property to prefer under the new rules

- Prioritize **new supply with real owner-occupier demand** and constrained competing stock.
- Avoid large, highly substitutable estates where ongoing land release can cap resale growth.
- Treat negative gearing as a secondary tailwind, not the core investment thesis.

## Bottom line
At your current income, a disciplined strategy is **one property now, second later only if real cashflow confirms it**. A two-property plan is feasible only with active discretionary-spend control and careful asset selection. A three-plus strategy is not recommended at current settings.
